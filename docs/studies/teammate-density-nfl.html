What this study asks

Instead of evaluating leagues alone, this study evaluates team-season environments. For each player in an alt-football team-season, we estimate the share of teammates who eventually appear in NFL records, then test whether players in denser NFL-bound environments convert to the NFL at higher rates.

How to read the deciles

Each player gets an average NFL-teammate density score across their alt-football team-seasons. We split players into deciles from D1 (lowest density) to D10 (highest density), then compute eventual NFL conversion rate within each bin.

Team-season incubators

The table ranks team-seasons by conversion lift relative to their own league baseline. This isolates unusually productive environments from leagues that are already NFL-heavy overall.

Methodology

Data source: player files under docs/data/players/ and league-season metadata in docs/data/sports.json.

Team-season identity is inferred from roster appearances keyed by (sport_id, team). Only non-NFL football leagues are included in environment construction. A player's NFL outcome is whether their record includes NFL in sport_names.

Because this is observational, results should be read as association, not proof that teammate quality causes NFL conversion.
